      @justusethisone: Not trying to lecture or anything, just a little FYI:

      First in this situation it is not the judges/courts adding modifiers; defiant trespassing is codified in many states by the state legislature, including in Pennsylvania under 18 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 3503.

      This varies from state to state as it is tort and criminal law, but many times defiant trespassing is used to distinguish between simple trespassers who enter property knowing they should not be there, and people who enter property where the owner has taken actual, affirmative steps to give notice of the trespass. This can include fences, structures, signs etc.

      Taking this baseball incident, this young man had to jump a wall built around the entire field - a step taken by the property owners to show boundaries and give notice that not everyone is permitted on the field. Secondly you can bet that at any major league ball park, various signs, announcements, and notices on the back of the tickets, spell out the rules regarding trespassing.

      Like I said many times these "modifiers" are important to distinguish the severity of one crime or act from another. It is no different than how states have Simple Assault criminal laws, and then more specific assaults (i.e. Assault on a Handicapped Person, on a Government Official, on a Pregnant Woman, etc.).
